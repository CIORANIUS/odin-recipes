HAMBURGUER RECIPE

HAMBURGUER PREPARATION METHOD

  1. Prepare the Meat Mixture:

    • n a large bowl, combine ground beef (80/20 lean-to-fat ratio is ideal for juicy burgers) with salt, pepper, and any additional seasonings or ingredients you prefer (e.g., garlic powder, onion powder, Worcestershire sauce, or finely chopped onions).

  2. Form the Patties:

    • Divide the meat mixture into equal portions, typically around 4-6 ounces each.
    • Gently shape each portion into a patty, making sure not to compress the meat too much. This keeps the burgers tender.
    • Use your thumb to create a small indent in the center of each patty to prevent it from puffing up during cooking.

  3. Cook the Patties:

    • Preheat your grill or a skillet over medium-high heat. Lightly oil the grill grates or the skillet.
    • Place the patties on the grill or skillet. Cook for about 3-4 minutes per side for medium doneness, or until they reach your desired level of doneness. Avoid pressing down on the patties with a spatula, as this squeezes out the juices.

  4. Toast the Buns:

    • While the patties are cooking, lightly toast the hamburger buns on the grill or in a toaster until golden brown.

  5. Assemble the Hamburgers:

    • Place a cooked patty on the bottom half of each toasted bun.
    • Add your choice of cheese (cheddar, American, Swiss, etc.) if desired, letting it melt over the patty.
    • Top with desired condiments and toppings such as lettuce, tomato slices, pickles, onions, ketchup, mustard, and mayonnaise.
    • Cover with the top half of the bun.

  6. Serve:

    • Serve the hamburgers immediately while hot, accompanied by your favorite sides like fries, chips, or a salad.
