COFFE ICE

is a delicious frozen dessert with a rich coffee flavor. It's made by infusing cream or milk with coffee beans or espresso, along with sugar, and then churning and freezing the mixture. The result is a creamy, slightly sweet, and slightly bitter treat with a delightful coffee aroma. Coffee ice cream is often enjoyed on its own or as a complement to other desserts, and it's a favorite among coffee lovers for its combination of coolness and the invigorating taste of coffee.

Ingredients

  • Coffee
  • Milk
  • Heavy cream
  • Sugar
  • Eggs yolks
  • Vanilla extract

Preparation steps

  1. Ingredients Preparation: Combine coffee, milk, and heavy cream in a saucepan. Heat the mixture until it's hot but not boiling.
  2. Mix Sugar and Egg Yolks: In a separate bowl, whisk together sugar and egg yolks until the mixture is pale and slightly thick.
  3. Temper Eggs: Gradually add a small amount of the hot coffee mixture into the egg mixture, whisking continuously to avoid curdling. This process is called tempering.
  4. Combine Mixtures: Pour the egg mixture back into the saucepan with the remaining coffee mixture and cook over low heat, stirring constantly until it thickens slightly (do not let it boil).
  5. Strain and Cool: Strain the mixture to remove any solids and let it cool to room temperature.
  6. Add Vanilla: Stir in vanilla extract to the cooled mixture.
  7. Chill: Cover the mixture and refrigerate for several hours or overnight until it's completely cold
  8. Churn and Freeze: Churn the mixture in an ice cream maker according to the manufacturer's instructions.
  9. Serve or Store: Serve immediately as soft-serve ice cream, or transfer to a container and freeze for a few hours until firm.
